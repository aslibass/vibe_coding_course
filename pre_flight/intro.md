# 🚀 PRE-FLIGHT: Week 0 Tech Readiness

Welcome to the **Vibe Coding Course**! We are so excited to have you on this journey. Over the next four weeks, you're going to learn how to move from zero coding experience to building and deploying real, helpful applications—what we call "Point Solutions"—using the power of AI.

Before we gather for our first session, we need to make sure your "digital toolkit" is packed and ready to go. Don't worry if these terms sound unfamiliar! We will guide you step-by-step.

This is your **Pre-Flight Checklist**. Please complete these steps before Session 1 so we can hit the ground running!

---

## 🛠️ Your Digital Toolkit

To build incredible things, we only need three main tools:
1. **A Web Browser**: To access our tools (Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge recommended).
2. **Google AI Studio**: Our intelligent assistant that will write the code for us.
3. **GitHub**: Our digital filing cabinet where we will store and share our projects.

---

## ✅ Step 1: Verify Your Google Account

We will use Google AI Studio as our primary "coding partner." You simply need a standard Google (Gmail) account to access it.

1. Go to [aistudio.google.com](https://aistudio.google.com/).
2. Click **Sign In** in the top right corner.
3. Sign in using your existing Google account (the one you use for Gmail or YouTube).
4. **Success Check:** Once signed in, you should see a welcoming screen with a chat area where you can talk to the AI. If you see this, you are ready!

*Note: If you are asked to accept Terms of Service, please read and accept them.*

---

## ✅ Step 2: Create Your GitHub Account

GitHub is where the world stores its code. Think of it as a public library for software. For us, it's where we will keep our work safe and share our deployed apps with the community.

1. Go to [github.com](https://github.com/).
2. Click the **Sign up** button in the top right corner.
3. Follow the friendly, step-by-step prompts:
   - Enter your email address.
   - Create a strong password.
   - Choose a unique username (Use your name or something professional, like `janesmith-apps`).
4. Verify your account (they usually send a puzzle to prove you are human, and an email to verify your address).
5. **Success Check:** Once you are logged into your GitHub dashboard, you are good to go!
